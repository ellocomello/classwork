Software Development Life Cycle

The systematic process used to design, develop, and test high-quality software

Agile Description

Agile is an approach that divides work into phases, emphasizing continuous delivery and improvement.

Iteration a software development model that breaks a project into smaller, manageable cycles called iterations, rather than building the entire system at once
Release the deployment of a software product or update to end-users in a production environment, making it accessible to the public
Maintenance the final stage of the SDLC where software is monitored, updated, and corrected after deployment to ensure it continues to function as intended and meets user needs
Retirement the final stage where a software system is taken out of production and decommissioned because it is obsolete, no longer supported, or being replaced by a new system

UX/UI designers describe, create products that are both aesthetically pleasing and easy to use, focusing on a user-centered approach throughout the entire product development lifecycle.
